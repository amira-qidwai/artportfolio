HI, I'M AMIRA QIDWAI

(A-mee-ra Kid-why)

Iâ€™m an artist and student at the University of Iowa. I use my experience with a variety of media to explore the intersection of art with international politics and geographic development.

I was born and raised in Iowa City, Iowa where I now attend college. My roots as a Midwesterner and Iowan influence my artwork, as does my experience of being half white and half Pakistani. Originally, my artwork was focused primarily on my heritage, but through investigating Pakistani culture, I found that the connections between all South Asian cultures interested me. Gradually, I became most interested in the unification and division of cultures and societies around the world.

I am currently a junior at the University of Iowa where I study international relations, geographic information science (GIS), and Arabic. My areas of study and my focus in art are aligned and inform each other. I enjoy using GIS technologies to supplement my artistic investigations of global unity and division. An example of this is my in-progress portfolio focusing on regional divisions in the United Nations. The research for this portfolio utilizes several geographic visualizations created using GIS technologies.
